# Accessibility Rubric (commercial_interiors)

## physical_access
- Accessible Routes (conf 1.00) - A barrier-free path of travel must be provided between accessible entrances and designated parking, passenger-loading zones, and public thoroughfares.
- Exterior Building Entrances (conf 1.00) - Universal washrooms require a power-operated door along a barrier-free path of travel.
- Automatic Door Operation (conf 1.00) - Power door operators must activate automatically or by accessible controls, be marked, and be operable at specified heights.
- Door Minimum Width (conf 1.00) - Doorways in barrier-free paths must have a clear width of at least 850 mm when open.
- Alternate Accessible Routes (Stair Alternatives) (conf 1.00) - Where escalators or inclined moving walks provide access to a floor, an interior barrier-free path of travel (e.g., elevator, lift) must also be provided to that floor.
- Mobility Device Space in Meeting Rooms (conf 1.00) - Assembly occupancies and waiting rooms with fixed seats must provide a minimum number of designated wheelchair spaces as per Table 3.8.2.3.
- Accessible Viewing Space Dimensions (conf 1.00) - Accessible viewing spaces in assembly areas must provide a minimum independent seating space of 850 mm by 1390 mm.
- Maintain Required Aisle Width Behind Accessible Seating (conf 1.00) - Aisle width behind accessible seating areas must be maintained to ensure unobstructed passage.
- Limits of Protruding Objects (conf 1.00) - Protruding building elements within 1980 mm of the floor must not project more than 100 mm horizontally into paths of travel unless clearance below is less than 680 mm.
- Wheel Stops to Prevent Vehicle Intrusion (conf 1.00) - Wheel stops should be used to prevent vehicles from intruding onto the barrier-free path of travel on sidewalks.
- Curb Ramp Detectability (conf 1.00) - Curbs and curb ramps should be designed with colour or texture contrast to be easily detected by people with low vision, blindness, or cognitive impairments.
- Ramps (conf 1.00) - Changes in level greater than 13 mm in a barrier-free path must be provided with sloped floors or ramps.
## neurodivergent
- Clear Wayfinding Signage (conf 1.00) - Clear signage indicating the location of accessible washrooms, elevators, and accessible routes must be provided and be easily visible.
- Pattern and Colour Blocking for Key Access (conf 1.00) - Visual and tactile or textural warning systems should be provided on escalator steps and floor surfaces at the top and bottom of escalators for orientation.
- Braille, Visual, and Audio Cues (conf 0.95) - Building directories should be tactile, on a sloping plane 760–900 mm above the floor, with characters raised at least 0.7 mm.
- Non-Text Diagrams and Symbols (conf 0.95) - Signs must incorporate the International Symbol of Access or the International Symbol of Access for Hearing Loss and appropriate graphical or textual information.
- Tactile Signs for Doors and Openings (conf 0.95) - Doors and openings from public places should be identified by tactile signs with letters ≥60 mm high, raised 0.7 mm, located 1200 mm above floor, ≤150 mm from door.
- Tactile Signage Requirements (conf 0.95) - Tactile signage must be not less than 60 mm high, raised ~0.7 mm, located ≤1200 mm above floor, ≤150 mm from entrance, and contrasting in colour.
- Accessible Control Mounting Height (conf 0.90) - Controls must be mounted 900 mm to 1 200 mm above the floor and adjacent to a clear floor space of 1 350 mm by 800 mm.
- Accessible Control Operation (conf 0.90) - Controls must be operable with one hand in a closed fist position, without tight grasping, pinching, or twisting, and require no more than 22 N of force.
- Haptic and Tactile Maps (conf 0.90) - Directions for blind persons can use pre-recorded messages or tactile maps and signs.
- High Colour Contrast for Orientation Signs (conf 0.90) - Identification of floors and orientation signs for visually disabled users should offer maximum colour contrast, preferably white on black or black on white.
- Lighting, Audible, Tactile, Colour/Contrast, and Ergonomic Cues (conf 0.90) - Orientation for visually disabled persons should use lighting, audible, tactile, colour/contrast, and ergonomic cues for wayfinding and safety.
- Quiet Space Availability (conf 0.90) - Ensure sufficient space and quiet space options are provided, including a small table for communication devices, to accommodate persons with neurodiversity and communication/cognitive disorders.
## sensory_alerts
- Audible and Visual Alerts (conf 1.00) - If an entrance is equipped with a security system, both visual and audible signals must indicate when the door lock is released.
- Warning Indicators (conf 1.00) - Downward changes in elevation in a barrier-free path must be signaled by tactile attention indicator surfaces per CSA B651.
- Tactile Attention Indicator Configuration (conf 1.00) - Tactile attention indicator surfaces must use truncated domes 4–5 mm high, top diameter 12–25 mm, base diameter 10±1 mm greater, in a square grid, with specified spacing.
- Tactile Attention Indicator Installation (conf 1.00) - Tactile attention indicator surfaces must be installed along the full width of the hazard, 600–650 mm deep, with one side against the edge of the hazard.
- Visual Contrast Between Walls and Floors (conf 1.00) - Tactile attention indicator surfaces must have a luminance (colour) contrast of at least 50% with adjacent surfaces using the Michelson contrast formula.
- Tactile Direction Indicator Configuration (conf 1.00) - Tactile direction indicator surfaces must use flat-topped, parallel, elongated bars 4–5 mm high, top width 17–30 mm, base width top+10±1 mm, top length ≥270 mm.
- Fire Alarm Sound Pressure Levels (conf 0.96) - Fire alarm signal sound pressure level must not exceed 110 dBA in any normally occupied area; in sleeping rooms, not less than 75 dBA with doors closed.
- Visual Signal Device Installation (conf 0.96) - Visual signal devices must be installed in addition to audible devices in buildings with fire alarm systems, conforming to CAN/ULC-S526, and be visible within suites.
- Assistive Listening at Service Counters (conf 0.95) - At least one service counter in assembly occupancy buildings must have an assistive listening system or adaptive technology, and amplification if there is a communication barrier.
- Assistive Listening Systems in Public Areas (conf 0.95) - Install assistive listening devices (ALDs) in all areas where occupancy might be 50 persons or more; public areas requiring PA systems for safety must have magnetic induction loop systems.
- Induction Loop Receiver Ratio (conf 0.95) - Where an induction loop system is deployed, provide 1 loop receiver for every 50 occupants for those without compatible hearing aids.
- Annual ALD and Loop System Testing (conf 0.95) - All ALDs and induction loop installations must be checked once per year and a Certificate of Conformity issued in accordance with IEC60118-4 2014 Standard.
## social_health
- Adult Changing Facility (conf 0.98) - Adult change tables must have a minimum clear floor space of 900 by 1830 mm in front, and be height adjustable from 450–500 mm (low) to 850–900 mm (high).
- Multilingual Signage (conf 0.90) - Standards must be available in multiple formats, including plain-language, ASL, and LSQ summaries, to ensure accessibility for diverse users.
